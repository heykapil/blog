## Billinear forms

1. Write down the following billinear forms in vector matrix notation $uAv^{T}$.
    - $\phi(u,v) = 3x_1y_1 -2x_1y_3 +5x_2y_1+7x_2y_2-8x_2y_3+4x_3y_2-6x_3y_3$.
    - $\phi(u,v) = -5x_1y_1 +6x_1y_2 -2x_1y_3+3x_2y_2-6x_2y_3$.
    - $\phi(u,v) = 2x_1y_3 -3x_3y_1+4x_3y_4$.
    - $\phi(u,v) = 4x_1y_1+2x_1y_2-2x_2y_1+3x_2y_2$.
    - $\phi(u,v) = 2x_1y_1-3x_1y_3+2x_2y_2$.
2. Find the matrix $A$ of bilinear form $\phi$ in basis $S$ and matrix $B$ of $\phi$ in basis $S^{'}$. Also, find the change of basis matrix $P$ from $S^{'}$ to $S$ and verify that $B=P^T AP$.
    -   $$ \phi(u,v) = 5x_1y_1 -3x_1y_2 -3x_2y_1+ 8x_2y_2 .$$ 

        $$ S = \{(1,1),(1,-1)\} \text{ and } S^{'}=\{(1,2),(2,1)\}. $$
    -   $$ \phi(u,v) = 4x_1y_1 -3x_1y_2 + 2x_2y_1 .$$ 

        $$ S = \{(1,0),(1,2)\} \text{ and } S^{'}=\{(0,1),(1,1)\} .$$
3. Prove that for any $n$-dimensional vector space $V$ over field $F$,

    $$ \phi_{i,j} (u,v) = f_i (u) f_j (v)   \text{ for all } u, v \in V $$

    Then set $\{\phi_{i,j} \mid i,j=1,2,3,\cdots ,n \}$ forms a basis of space $B(V)$ of bilinear forms on $V$ , 
    where $\{f_i \mid i=1,2,3,\cdots,n \}$ is a basis of dual space $V^{*}$.
